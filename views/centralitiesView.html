Degree

Degree centrality measures the importance of a node by the degree of the node, i.e. the number of edges incident to the node. The higher degree of a node, the more important the node is.
Wikipedia: degree (graph theory)[ https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Degree_(graph_theory) ]

Closeness

Closeness centrality quantifies importance of a node in terms of shortest distances from this node to all other nodes in the graph. The most important node is the one with the shortest average path length to all other nodes.
Wikipedia: centrality [ https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centrality ]

Betweenness

Betweenness centrality quantifies the importance of a given node in the context of network flow. If we consider all the shortest paths in the network, then the greater the proportion of such paths that goes through a given node, the more important the role of that node in the network.
Wikipedia: centrality [ https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centrality ]

Eigevector Centrality

Eigenvector centrality "assigns relative scores to all nodes in the network based on the concept that connections to high-scoring nodes contribute more to the score of the node in question than equal connections to low-scoring nodes."
Wikipedia: centrality [ https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centrality ]
