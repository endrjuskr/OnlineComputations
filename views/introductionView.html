Computational Toolbox

In network analysis and graph theory, centrality measures identify the most important nodes in a network (graph). Among numerous applications of centrality measures, there are such classic examples as identifying the most important hubs in a road network (Schultes & Sanders, 2007), the most critical functional entities in a protein network (Jeong, Mason, Barabasi, & Oltvai, 2001), or the most influential people in a social network (Kempe, Kleinberg, & Tardos, 2003).

This applet is a free-tool to compute and compare various centrality measures on-line. At the moment, it includes the following centrality measures:

  • classic centrality measures such as degree, closeness, betweenness and eigenvector

  • more advanced centrality measures based on game-theoretic concepts such as the Shapley value and the Myerson value. For more information on game-theoretic centrality measures click here .

We constantly enrich our software library with new measures. If you are interested in a specific measure that is not yet included in our on-line applet please write to tomasz.michalak<<@>>cs.ox.ac.uk.
